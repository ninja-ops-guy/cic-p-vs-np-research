# Section 1: Literature Review

## Historical Context

### Early Work
- **Cook (1971)**: Proved SAT is NP-complete
- **Levin (1973)**: Independently discovered NP-completeness
- **Karp (1972)**: Showed 21 problems are NP-complete

### Approaches to P vs NP

#### Circuit Complexity
- **Shannon (1949)**: Counting argument for circuit lower bounds
- **Razborov (1985)**: Monotone circuit lower bounds
- **Williams (2011)**: ACC lower bounds via satisfiability algorithms

#### Proof Complexity
- **Cook-Reckhow (1979)**: Formalized proof systems
- **Haken (1985)**: Exponential lower bounds for resolution
- **Razborov (1995)**: Lower bounds for polynomial calculus

#### Algebraic Geometry
- **Mulmuley-Sohoni (2001)**: Geometric Complexity Theory (GCT)
- **Attempts to use representation theory for lower bounds

#### Information Theory
- **Chaitin (1960s-70s)**: Algorithmic information theory
- **Various attempts to connect information and complexity

## Current State
Despite tremendous progress, P vs NP remains open. No single approach has succeeded, suggesting that new ideas are needed.

## The CIC Contribution
The Computational Information Complexity framework synthesizes insights from multiple approaches:
- Information theory for measuring complexity
- Graph structure for characterizing instances
- Proof complexity for lower bounds
- Machine learning for practical algorithms
