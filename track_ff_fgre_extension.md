# Track FF: Frege Extension

## Overview
Extension of the CIC framework to Frege and Extended Frege proof systems.

## Frege Systems
Frege (also called Hilbert-style) proof systems are the strongest "standard" propositional proof systems.

## Extended Frege
Extended Frege adds the ability to introduce new variables as abbreviations for formulas.

## Key Results

### Theorem FF-1
If EF has polynomial-size proofs for all tautologies, then P = NP.

### Theorem FF-2
The CIC of EF proofs is at most the CIC of Frege proofs.

### Theorem FF-3 (Conjecture)
EF does not have polynomial-size proofs for all tautologies.

## Implications
Frege and Extended Frege are at the frontier of proof complexity:
- Lower bounds would imply P != NP
- They capture most known algorithms
- Understanding their power is crucial
