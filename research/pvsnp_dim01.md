# P vs NP Dimension 1: Uniform vs Non-uniform Complexity

## Overview
Analysis of uniform (Turing machine) vs non-uniform (circuit) complexity and implications for P vs NP.

## Uniform Complexity
- **P**: Polynomial time on a Turing machine
- **NP**: Nondeterministic polynomial time

## Non-uniform Complexity
- **P/poly**: Polynomial-size circuits
- **NP/poly**: Non-uniform NP

## Key Results

### Theorem 1.1
P subset P/poly (trivial).

### Theorem 1.2
If NP not subset P/poly, then P != NP.

### Theorem 1.3 (Karp-Lipton)
If NP subset P/poly, then PH = Sigma_2 P.

## Implications
- Circuit lower bounds imply P != NP
- Non-uniform lower bounds may be easier to prove
- Karp-Lipton shows consequences of NP having circuits
