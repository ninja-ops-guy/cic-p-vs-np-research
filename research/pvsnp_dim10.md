# P vs NP Dimension 10: Algebraic Approaches

## Overview
Algebraic approaches to P vs NP, including Geometric Complexity Theory (GCT).

## Geometric Complexity Theory

### Foundational Idea
Use algebraic geometry and representation theory to prove complexity lower bounds.

### Key Players
- Ketan Mulmuley
- Milind Sohoni

### The Approach
1. Associate algebraic varieties to complexity classes
2. Show that inclusions of classes correspond to morphisms of varieties
3. Use representation theory to prove non-existence of such morphisms

## Key Results

### Theorem 10.1 (Mulmuley-Sohoni)
The permanent vs determinant problem can be studied via orbit closures in algebraic geometry.

### Theorem 10.2
The GCT approach requires solving deep problems in representation theory.

## Obstacles

### The "Flip" Problem
Need to find positive representations of lower bounds.

### Representation Theory Barriers
The required representation-theoretic results are extremely difficult.

## The CIC Connection

### Information Geometry
CIC connects to algebraic approaches through information geometry:
- Statistical manifolds of formula distributions
- Fisher information metric on problem instances
- Geometric characterization of hardness

## Assessment
GCT is a deep and promising approach but remains far from resolving P vs NP.
