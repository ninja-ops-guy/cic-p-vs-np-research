# The P = NP Landscape

## Overview
A survey of the landscape of approaches to proving or disproving P = NP.

## Approaches to Prove P = NP

### 1. Direct Algorithm
Find a polynomial-time algorithm for SAT.
- **Status**: No such algorithm is known
- **Difficulty**: Would require fundamentally new ideas

### 2. Algebraic Methods
Use algebraic geometry or representation theory.
- **Status**: GCT is the main approach, but very difficult
- **Difficulty**: Requires deep mathematical breakthroughs

### 3. Logical Methods
Use proof theory or model theory.
- **Status**: Limited progress
- **Difficulty**: Logical barriers exist

## Approaches to Prove P != NP

### 1. Circuit Lower Bounds
Show NP requires superpolynomial circuits.
- **Status**: Best known is roughly n^3 for specific problems
- **Difficulty**: Natural Proofs barrier

### 2. Proof Complexity
Show SAT requires superpolynomial proofs.
- **Status**: Good lower bounds for weak proof systems
- **Difficulty**: Hard for strong systems like Frege

### 3. Complexity of Search
Show that search is harder than decision.
- **Status**: Partial results
- **Difficulty**: Need better understanding of search complexity

## The CIC Approach
The Computational Information Complexity framework offers a new perspective:
- Information complexity as a fundamental measure
- Structural analysis of instances
- New lower bound techniques

## Assessment
No approach has succeeded yet. The problem remains one of the deepest open questions in mathematics.
