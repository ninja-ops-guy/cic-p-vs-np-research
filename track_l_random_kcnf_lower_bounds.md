# Track L: Random k-CNF Lower Bounds

## Overview
Lower bounds for solving random k-CNF formulas.

## Random k-CNF Model
A random k-CNF formula with n variables and m clauses is generated by:
1. Selecting k variables uniformly at random
2. Negating each variable with probability 1/2
3. Forming a clause and adding it to the formula

## Key Results

### Theorem L-1
Random 3-CNF formulas at density > 4.51 are unsatisfiable with high probability.

### Theorem L-2
Any DPLL algorithm requires exponential time on random 3-CNF at the threshold.

### Theorem L-3
The treewidth of random k-CNF is Theta(n) with high probability.

## Phase Transition
| Density | Satisfiability | Typical Treewidth |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| < 3.52 | Mostly SAT | O(log n) |
| 3.52-4.51 | Mixed | O(n) |
| > 4.51 | Mostly UNSAT | Theta(n) |

## Implications
Random instances near the satisfiability threshold are both structurally and computationally complex.
