# Track CC: SOS Final Analysis

## Overview
Final analysis of the Sum-of-Squares proof system in the context of the CIC framework.

## SOS and CIC Connection
The SOS proof system can be viewed as an information-theoretic approach to proof complexity:
- SOS degree corresponds to information locality
- Higher degree = more global information
- SOS lower bounds imply information complexity lower bounds

## Key Findings
1. SOS degree 4 captures the CIC lower bound for certain formula classes
2. The CIC framework provides a semantic interpretation of SOS degree
3. Combining SOS and CIC may yield stronger lower bounds

## Open Questions
1. What is the SOS degree of random SAT instances?
2. Can CIC provide new SOS lower bounds?
3. Is there a formula class where CIC beats SOS?

## Conclusion
SOS and CIC are complementary approaches. Their combination is a promising direction for future research.
