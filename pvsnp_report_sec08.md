# Section 8: Open Problems and Future Directions

## Open Problems

### Problem 1: Width-Complexity Connection
Prove (or disprove) that SAT requires exponential time when the formula has high treewidth.

**Status**: Conjectured, partial results known.

### Problem 2: Natural Proofs Circumvention
Show that the CIC framework genuinely circumvents the Natural Proofs barrier.

**Status**: Argument is suggestive but incomplete.

### Problem 3: Complete Propagation Tightness Proof
Remove the bounded-width assumption from the propagation tightness theorem.

**Status**: Under active investigation.

### Problem 4: Extension Beyond SAT
Extend the CIC framework to other NP-complete problems.

**Status**: Preliminary work on TSP and graph coloring.

## Future Directions

### Direction 1: Stronger Lower Bounds
Develop new proof techniques for information complexity lower bounds.

### Direction 2: Practical Algorithms
Continue developing SAT solvers that exploit structural insights.

### Direction 3: Quantum Extensions
Explore quantum information complexity and its implications.

### Direction 4: Machine Learning
Use ML to discover new complexity measures and relationships.

1. Publish core theorems in peer-reviewed venues
2. Release SAT solvers for community evaluation
